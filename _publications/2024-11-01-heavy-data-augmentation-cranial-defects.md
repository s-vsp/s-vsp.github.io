## Abstract

This work studies how stronger and more diverse data augmentation can improve automatic reconstruction of cranial defects for personalized implant modeling. It compares classical geometric transformations, image registration, variational autoencoders, generative adversarial models, and latent diffusion models. Heavy augmentation improves both quantitative results and visual quality, with latent diffusion and vector-quantized autoencoding emerging as especially effective for creating heterogeneous synthetic training data. The augmented models also reconstruct real clinical defects without requiring costly annotations for those cases.
