## Abstract

This work addresses skull segmentation from MR images, where bone is difficult to recover because MRI primarily visualizes soft tissue. The method reframes the task as modality translation: MR volumes are transformed into CT-like data, then segmented using CT-oriented processing. The study compares several generative translation strategies, including adversarial, diffusion, and contrastive learning methods, and adds a super-resolution stage to support high-resolution clinical workflows. The resulting pipeline enables fast volumetric inference and improves over supervised baselines, foundation-model segmentation, and alternative translation approaches.
