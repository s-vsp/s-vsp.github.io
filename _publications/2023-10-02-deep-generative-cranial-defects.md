## Abstract

This paper studies synthetic data generation for personalized cranial implant design, where the range of possible defects is broad and real annotated data are limited. It compares volumetric WGAN-GP, VAE-pretrained WGAN-GP, and IntroVAE models for generating synthetic defective skulls and corresponding defect regions. The generated samples are evaluated through latent-space analysis and a downstream V-Net segmentation task. The results show that synthetic augmentation can substantially improve segmentation compared with training only on the original data.
