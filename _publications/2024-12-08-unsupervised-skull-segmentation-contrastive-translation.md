## Abstract

The paper tackles skull segmentation from MR scans, a harder setting than CT because bone structures are weakly represented in MRI. Instead of directly segmenting MR images or relying on expensive skull annotations, the method translates MR data into synthetic CT-like volumes and performs segmentation in that space. The approach uses contrastive learning for unpaired MR and CT datasets, includes super-resolution for low-quality or low-resolution inputs, and studies generalization to downstream medical planning tasks.
